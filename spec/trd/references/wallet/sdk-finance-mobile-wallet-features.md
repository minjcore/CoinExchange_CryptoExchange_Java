The global mobile wallet app market is growing rapidly and is expected to continue to grow in the coming years. With the rise of digital payments and increasing use of mobile devices, there is a high demand for mobile wallet apps. According to a recent [market research,](https://www.juniperresearch.com/press/digital-wallet-users-exceed-5bn-globally-2026) the global mobile wallet app market was valued at $1.06 billion in 2020 and is expected to reach $4.91 billion by 2026, growing at a CAGR of 31.4% between 2021 and 2026. The growth of the market is driven by several factors, including popularity of mobile devices, adoption of digital payments, and awareness of the benefits of mobile wallets.

In addition, the COVID -19 pandemic has accelerated the shift towards contactless and digital payments, further driving the growth of the mobile wallet app market. The rise of e-commerce and popularity of mobile payment methods such as QR code-based payments and NFC payments are also contributing to the growth of the market. Overall, the global mobile wallet apps market is poised for significant growth in the coming years and offers a promising opportunity for players in the mobile payments industry.

## What are the different types of mobile wallets?

There are several types of mobile wallets, including:

|     |     |
| --- | --- |
| Digital wallets | These allow users to store their credit or debit card information, as well as loyalty cards and other types of digital information, on their mobile devices. Examples include Apple Pay, Google Pay, and Samsung Pay. |
| Peer-to-peer (P2P) payment apps | These allow users to send and receive money directly from one another using their mobile devices. Examples include Venmo, PayPal, and Zelle. |
| Mobile banking apps | These software systems allow users to access their bank accounts and perform transactions, such as check balances, pay bills, and transfer money, directly from their mobile devices. |
| Retail store mobile wallets | These allow users to store loyalty card information, coupons, and other types of information from specific retail stores on their mobile devices. |
| Cryptocurrency wallets | These allow users to store and manage their cryptocurrency holdings, such as Bitcoin and Ethereum, on their mobile devices. |

Overall, the type of mobile wallet a person uses will depend on their individual needs and preferences, as well as the type of transactions they need to perform.

## What are the must-have features of a viable digital wallet in 2025?

Nowadays, users and especially the young, digitally raised generations expect the best experience when it comes to their financial lives. Some of them have forgotten about traditional physical wallets and use smartphones to process payments faster. For this reason, customers are looking for cutting-edge, highly secure financial solutions that make payments as easy as possible.

Banks, financial firms, telecom providers and e-commerce companies can no longer afford to ignore the mobile financial services revolution, especially mobile wallet capabilities. They also need to be more innovative than ever to offer outstanding mobile wallet features that ensure not only high security but also a great customer experience.

### Here are some of the must-have features of a future-ready digital wallet in 2025:

#### **Enhanced security**

Mobile wallets are generally considered a more secure option than physical debit or credit cards because of their powerful encryption. However, many people are still concerned about the security of digital wallets, with 93% of respondents citing fear of hacking as the main reason holding them back from further use. To ensure security, digital wallets rely on proven security measures such as encryption and tokenization that protect user data from being accessed by unauthorized parties. In this regard, security should be the top priority for any digital wallet. This can be achieved through features such as biometric authentication, encryption of sensitive data, and multi-factor authentication.

Read more: [How to Create a Digital Wallet: Key Features and Solutions](https://sdk.finance/blog/how-to-create-a-digital-wallet-key-features-and-solutions/)

#### **Support for multiple currencies**

A mobile wallet that can store multiple currencies, including cryptocurrencies and fiat currencies, is more versatile and convenient for users. Virtual wallets are more convenient for users because they allow transferring payments and funds to different currency accounts in a single application, without the need to maintain a separate account for each international currency.

#### **Decentralized ID (DID) integration**

Decentralized Identity (DID) is a concept where individuals gain control over their digital identity through wallets. DID Integration with mobile wallets allows users to store their digital identity and credentials securely and encrypted. By integrating decentralized identity solutions, users have more control over their personal data and have a single identity point for multiple services.

## Things to consider before starting a mobile wallet app

Starting a mobile wallet app requires careful planning and execution to succeed in a competitive market. By considering essential factors, you can set your mobile wallet app on the path to success. However, it’s important to remain flexible and adapt to changes in the market and technology. Here are some important things to consider before launching a mobile wallet app:

### Regulatory requirements

Consider the laws and regulations related to mobile wallets in your target market. Here are some of the regulatory requirements for mobile wallets:

- Know Your Customer (KYC) Requirements: Mobile wallet providers must comply with KYC requirements to verify the identity of their users. This helps prevent fraud and money laundering.
- Data Protection: Mobile wallet providers must have measures in place to protect the personal and financial data of their users. This includes data encryption and regular security audits.
- Payment Card Industry Data Security Standards (PCI DSS): Mobile wallet providers that accept payment card transactions must comply with PCI DSS requirements, which are designed to protect cardholder data and prevent fraud.
- Anti-Money Laundering (AML) and Counter-Terrorism Financing (CTF) Requirements: Mobile wallet providers must have measures in place to prevent money laundering and the financing of terrorism.
- Consumer Protection: Mobile wallet providers must provide clear and concise information about their services, fees, and terms and conditions. They must also have a complaints procedure in place to deal with customer complaints and disputes.
- Interoperability: Some regulatory bodies may require mobile wallet providers to make their services interoperable with other payment systems to ensure that consumers have a choice of payment options.

The specific regulatory requirements for mobile wallets may vary depending on the country or region where the service is offered. It is important for mobile wallet providers to stay up to date with the latest regulatory developments and comply with all relevant requirements to ensure the security and reliability of their services.

### Payment integration

Payment integration for mobile wallets refers to the process of connecting mobile wallets to payment systems, such as credit cards, debit cards, bank accounts, or other payment networks, so that users can make purchases or transfer funds using their mobile devices. Here are some common payment integrations for mobile wallets:

- Card-based payments: Mobile wallets can integrate with credit and debit card networks, such as Visa or Mastercard, allowing users to make payments at merchants that accept card payments. To enable this integration, mobile wallets must comply with payment industry security standards, such as the Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS).
- Bank transfers: Mobile wallets can be integrated with bank accounts, allowing users to transfer funds between accounts using their mobile devices. This integration requires the mobile wallet to comply with bank regulations and security standards.
- Mobile carrier billing: Mobile wallets can be integrated with mobile network operators, allowing users to charge purchases to their mobile phone bill. This integration is popular in emerging markets where credit card penetration is low.
- Digital wallets: Mobile wallets can be integrated with other digital wallets, such as PayPal or Alipay, allowing users to transfer funds between accounts or make payments using their preferred digital wallet.
- QR code payments: Mobile wallets can be integrated with QR code payment systems, such as WeChat Pay or Paytm, allowing users to make payments by scanning a QR code at the merchant’s point of sale.

To implement payment integrations, mobile wallet providers need to have the appropriate technical infrastructure, security measures, and partnerships with payment service providers or financial institutions. It is important for mobile wallet providers to ensure that payment integrations are secure, reliable, and user-friendly to provide a seamless payment experience for their users.

## FAQ

### What is a mobile wallet?

A mobile wallet is a digital application that allows users to store, manage, and transact various forms of digital payments using a mobile device such as a smartphone or tablet. It enables users to make purchases, transfer money, pay bills, and access other financial services.

A mobile wallet typically stores payment information such as credit or debit card details, bank account information, or digital currencies like Bitcoin or Ethereum. Once the payment information is saved in the mobile wallet, the user can easily make transactions without having to enter their payment information repeatedly.
