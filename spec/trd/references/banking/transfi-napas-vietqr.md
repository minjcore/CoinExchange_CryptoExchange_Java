# Vietnam’s NAPAS & VietQR: The Future of Payments in Southeast Asia

Vietnam is rapidly emerging as one of the financial and digital leaders in Southeast Asia with its innovative bunch of payment rails which are designed to propagate financial inclusion and reduce reliance on cash. At the centre of this transformation are the core rails - Vietnam’s NAPAS (National payment corporation of Vietnam) and VietQR payments, which together are crucial to the infrastructure and are shaping the Vietnam payment system. As cashless adoption is accelerating in the country, understanding what NAPAS is in Vietnam and how VietQR works and accommodating payments becomes extremely important - understanding of which would help decode and build on the fintech ecosystem in the country which is essential for businesses, startups and global enterprises in the region.

## What is NAPAS?

NAPAS (National Payment Corporation of Vietnam) is the backbone of Vietnam’s digital payment ecosystem. It was established under the State bank of Vietnam, NAPAS serves as the national switch and clearing house for interbank transactions. It enables seamless seamless networks among banks, e-wallets and financial institutions. With over 50 banks and fintech partners, NAPAS connects millions of consumers and businesses to real time, secure payment networks. Key functions of NAPAS;

1. National switching for card and non card payments.

2. Interbank fund transfers and clearing services.

3. Enables real-time payments in Vietnam across banks and fintechs.

4. Supports cashless payment systems and governments vision towards a cashless society.

## Understanding VietQR

VietQR is Vietnam’s standardised payment system developed under NAPAS and the Vietnam Bank association. Let’s understand how VietQR works;

1. Customers simply scan the merchant’s VietQR code using their mobile banking app.

2. The app directly connects via NAPAS rails to initiate the transfer.

3. Funds are then instantly transferred from the customer’s bank account to the merchant’s bank account.

##### VietQR comes with a lot of advantages such as;

1. **Interoperability** \- it functions across multiple banks and wallets and not just a single provider.
2. **Speed** \- it accentuates the speed by providing real time transfers.

3. **Low cost** \- it reduces merchant and customer reliance on card networks which often come with high fees.

4. **Ubiquity**-VietQR’s adoption is expanding rapidly across retail, ecommerce and services.

## The role of NAPAS and VietQR in Vietnam’s payment system

1. Together, these two systems form the foundation of modern payment systems in Vietnam.

2. Where NAPAS provides rails and VietQR provides the connectivity and the interface for cashless transactions - the two systems bundle up as an end to end payment service.

3. They both support strong connectivity across Vietnam's banking and payments systems - banks, mobile wallets and payment platforms.

4. This model has successfully positioned itself as the potential leader in South Asia’s payment innovations.

**Growth drivers of digital payments in Vietnam**

1. **Government policies** \- The state Bank of Vietnam actively promotes cashless transactions making the foundation of payment innovation stronger.

2. **Consumer behavior** \- Young, mobile first population drives ewallet and digital payment adoption via banking apps and other digital initiatives.

3. **SME enablement** \- VietQR enables small merchants and industries of all sizes to adopt and accept cashless payments without heavy POS terminals.

4. **E-commerce expansion** \- Vietnam’s booming digital economy demands solutions to suit its digital needs of fast, secure and cost effective payment solutions.

5. **Fintech and banking collaborations** \- NAPAS ensures seamless integration between incumbents and startups.
