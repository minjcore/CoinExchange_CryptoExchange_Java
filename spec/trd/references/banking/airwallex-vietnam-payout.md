# Send money to Vietnam: cross-border payment guide for Hong Kong businesses

![](https://images.ctfassets.net/sxag7u4cz1re/4j0Cddd9VWGLMUXfU2QicA/57defcc548296e0c83c33769acba4694/Frame_2095585715.png?q=70&w=112&h=112&fit=fill&fm=jpg)

Vietnam has rapidly emerged as a fast-growing market for Hong Kong companies. Whether you're paying suppliers, sourcing materials, or managing local partners, transferring money ‌quickly and reliably has become a competitive necessity.

Businesses still relying on traditional bank wire transfers often fall short on speed, cost, and flexibility.

In this guide, we’ll walk you through the common ways to send money to Vietnam, highlight the difficulties associated with conventional methods, and show how Airwallex helps Hong Kong businesses streamline payments, reduce costs, and stay in control of cash flow.

## Why do businesses need to send money to Vietnam?

Over the past decade, Vietnam's rapid growth in manufacturing and technology has created huge opportunities. For Hong Kong businesses, this means needing to make payments for several reasons, including:

- **Manufacturing and sourcing:** Vietnam is a manufacturing powerhouse, thanks to its lower labour costs and strategic location. It's a go-to hub for Hong Kong companies looking to produce goods or source materials, making regular payments for goods and processing fees a core process of their business.

- **Outsourcing services:** As Vietnam's talent pool grows rapidly in areas like IT, design, and customer support, Hong Kong startups and SMEs are increasingly tapping into this expertise, making cross-border payments a regular operational task.

- **Market expansion:** As Vietnam’s consumer base expands, more Hong Kong brands are establishing a presence there. This requires handling everyday local expenses from afar, such as paying salaries, renting office space, and settling with suppliers.


Whether your business is B2B or B2C, a fast and cost-effective way to send money to Vietnam will save you time and resources.

## Common ways to transfer money to Vietnam

When it comes to sending a cross-border payment, Hong Kong businesses typically have these three main options:

- Traditional bank transfers (SWIFT)

- International remittance providers (e.g. Western Union)

- All-in-one financial platforms (e.g. Airwallex)


While all of these methods are viable, they differ greatly in speed, cost, exchange rates, and overall effectiveness.

## Limitations associated with conventional bank transfers

Many businesses still rely on their banks for international transfers. However, in today’s fast-paced environment, this approach is no longer as competitive as it once was.

- Higher fees: Traditional bank wire transfers often have fees that appear straightforward. However, payments routed through the SWIFT network can pass through multiple intermediary banks, each taking a small slice that quietly erodes the final amount your recipient receives.

- Lack of transparency in exchange rates: Banks typically build hidden margins into their foreign exchange rates. This means a seemingly decent rate can mask a profit cushion, which becomes an extra cost for you.

- Slow settlement times: Funds passing through multiple intermediaries can take 2–5 business days to arrive. If you miss the cut-off time or send money before a public holiday, delays can stretch even longer, potentially straining relationships with your suppliers.

- Cumbersome process: Many banks still require multiple forms and in-person visits during branch hours, which limits flexibility for busy businesses.


For companies handling frequent, high-value, or multi-currency payments, these inefficiencies directly impact cash flow and day-to-day operations.

- Fully online experience: The entire process, from account opening to making a payment, is managed online. You can initiate a payment anytime from your phone, with no queues or printed forms, saving you time and effort.

- One account, multiple currencies: Go beyond HKD and VND. You can transfer money in over 90 currencies from the same account, giving your business the flexibility to manage global funds from a single platform.
